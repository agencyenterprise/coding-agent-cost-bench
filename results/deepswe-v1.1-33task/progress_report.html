Two success rates — how to read them. Every task is attempted up to 4 times (independent runs).
Example — one task, its 4 attempts: ✗ ✗ ✗ ✓
  • pass@1 — single-shot success: the share of individual attempts that pass. This task = 1 of 4 = 25%. Pooled over every attempt it gives the per-run table below. This is the number leaderboards (SWE-bench, DeepSWE) usually report.
  • pass@k — best-of-k: whether the model solved the task in at least one of its attempts. This task = solved ✓ (it passed once). Pooled over tasks it gives the per-task table below. Higher, because retries are folded in.
Read it as: “with 4 attempts GLM-5.2 (high) solves 81% of tasks (pass@k); a single attempt succeeds 47% of the time (pass@1).”
